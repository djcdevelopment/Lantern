# Player-First UX Model

## The Core UX Question

The first screen should answer:

"What happened to me last night?"

NOT:
"What giant system am I looking at?"

---

# Desired Emotional Arc

## Step 1 — Recognition

The player immediately recognizes:
- their character
- their raid
- their participation
- their role
- their moments

---

## Step 2 — Reflection

The player explores:
- wipes
- mistakes
- successes
- support moments
- timeline flow
- progression context

---

## Step 3 — Expansion

The player naturally broadens outward:
- team behavior
- encounter replay
- raid patterns
- roster continuity

---

## Step 4 — Contribution

The player understands:
- how uploads help the guild
- how memory forms
- how retrospectives connect
- how contribution remains optional

---

# Important UX Constraint

Avoid:
- giant default dashboards
- analytics overload
- esports aesthetics
- excessive charts
- engagement loops

Prefer:
- narrative clarity
- inspectability
- progressive disclosure
- contextual detail
- meaningful replay entry points

---

# Important Community Signals

The UI should make room for:
- feast providers
- cauldron providers
- support players
- raid leaders
- assistant raid leaders
- alt swaps
- progression sacrifices
- continuity contributions

These are socially important signals.
